Graduate Students

Kelsey Kleinhans

Kelsey is a 4th year PhD Candidate from St. Louis, Missouri studying transport properties of knee meniscus tissues. In general, her work is looking at how different parameters affect the apparent glucose diffusion coefficient, electrical conductivity, glucose partition coefficient, and hydraulic permeability of healthy porcine knee meniscus tissues. Her overall goal is to investigate some baseline characteristics of meniscus in the hopes that one day research will be able to better understand how tissue degeneration leads to osteoarthritis in the knee and ways to prevent and/or treat this disease. She hopes to someday work designing athletic equipment and/or with safety testing for athletes. Outside of work, Kelsey loves to bake, is a huge sports fan, and enjoys running around the city of Miami.
