The Team

Principal Investigator

Dr. Alicia Jackson

Assistant Professor, Department of Biomedical Engineering

Dr. Jackson joined the faculty in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the University of Miami in August 2011. She received her PhD under Dr. Weiyong Gu in BME at UM in 2010 for her work on Transport and Metabolism of Glucose in Intervertebral Disc which combined both experimental and theoretical approaches. She also received her MSBE (2007) and BSBE (2005) degrees from UM BME. As the director of the Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory, Dr. Jackson is interested in characterizing the transport and electromechanical properties of the fibrocartilaginous meniscus of the knee, in order to more fully understand the tissue pathology and contribute to the development of new strategies for treatment. She is also interested in elucidating the roles of physicochemical cues (e.g., nutrition, mechanical loading) in the degeneration cascade in intervertebral disc tissues, with particular emphasis on the notochordal nucleus pulposus cells. Dr. Jackson currently teaches Biomedical Transport Phenomena (BME 450), a required course for all BME undergraduate students.
