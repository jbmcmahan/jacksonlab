Graduate Students

Students pursuing a masters or doctoral degree in Biomedical Engineering are encouraged to apply for a research position in the Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory.

Various types of masters programs are offered in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Miami - 5 year dual BS/MS as well as thesis and non-thesis options for students who have already obtained a bachelorâ€™s degree. No stipend or tuition remission is provided to masters students. Partial scholarships are provided for exceptional candidates.

The University of Miami College of Engineering offers excellent packages to qualified applicants to the doctoral program in Biomedical Engineering. The current stipend for graduate research associates is $27,000 for a 12 month appointment. Tuition remission and health insurance credits are also provided.

If you are interested in graduate research opportunities in the Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory, please contact Dr. Jackson.

Undergraduate Students

Undergraduate students are encouraged to work in biomedical laboratories as early in their career as possible. Students working in Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory participate in characterization of transport and electrical properties of cartilaginous tissues, apparatus design and testing, and investigating physiochemical effects on cells. Students are encouraged to present their projects at the University of Miami Research and Creativity Forum. Exceptional students are invited to present posters at international conferences.

If you are interested in working in the Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory, please send your resume by e-mail to Dr. Jackson.
