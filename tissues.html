Orthopaedic Tissue Research

Knee meniscus tissues are avascular tissues that assist in load bearing and shock absorption in the knee, amongst other functions. Due to its lack of vascularization, nutrients and fluids must find alternative means of reaching the inner areas of the meniscus. In this lab, we are studying different transport properties of knee meniscus tissues.

Some of the properties being investigated include: the apparent glucose diffusivity, electrical conductivity, the glucose partition coefficient, and hydraulic permeability.

Our long term goals are to (1) better understand the pathophysiology involved in the development of knee meniscus injury and degeneration; (2) explore the roles of solute and fluid transport in the nutritional supply and mechanical functioning of the meniscus in order to more fully understand the origins of meniscal degradation; and (3) identify effective treatment solutions for Osteoarthritis.
