Research

Our laboratory is dedicated to the investigation of transport properties and nutritional supply to cells and tissues along with the biomechanics of soft tissues. At the cellular level, we work to understand the behavior of cells and how changes in physiological conditions affect the degenerative state of tissues in the intervertebral disc. At the tissue level, we look at transport properties through knee meniscus tissues in order to understand the onset and causes of osteoarthritis.

Cellular Level

Alhough many tissue properties of intervertebral disc (IVD) have been studied, there are still gaps in the cellular behavior of IVD cells and how their behavior leads to degeneration. In this lab, we are studying the phenotypic expression of a host of different genes related to ECM regulation, as well as monitoring the metabolic activity of cells.

Tissue Level

Knee meniscus tissues are avascular tissues that assist in load bearing and shock absorption in the knee, amongst other functions. Due to its lack of vascularization, nutrients and fluids must find alternative means of reaching the inner areas of the meniscus. In this lab, we are studying different transport properties of knee meniscus tissues.
